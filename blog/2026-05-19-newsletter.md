This update brings a richer baseline correction toolkit and full typographic control over axes and spectrum information blocks.

1. **Baseline correction: five algorithms and live anchor control.** The baseline correction tool now offers five algorithms — airPLS, Polynomial, Whittaker, Cubic spline, and Bernstein — so you can match the method to your spectrum. Anchor points can be placed anywhere on the spectrum and dragged to fine-tune the result in real time, with the baseline updating live as you move them. [Read the documentation](/help/baseline).

![Baseline correction with live anchor points](/newsletters/2026/may/baseline.gif)

2. **Publication-ready exports — axis typography and grid control.** You can now set the font size, bold, and italic for axis labels and the spectrum information block directly in the interface — no graphics editor needed. Settings are saved in the `.nmrium.zip` file, so every export produces a consistent, publication-ready figure. Major and minor grid lines can also be shown, hidden, and fully customized — color, width, and style — in both 1D and 2D spectra. [Read the documentation](/help/customization).

![Axis typography and grid customization](/newsletters/2026/may/custom.gif)
